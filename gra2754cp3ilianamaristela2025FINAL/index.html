Assembling Ikebana

Start With a Base

Choose a shallow vase and place a kenzan (flower frog) inside to hold stems in place. Add a small amount of water.

Select Your Flowers & Branches

Pick three main elements: a tall branch for height (Shin), a medium flower (Soe), and a shorter flower or leaf (Hikae) to create balance.

Trim & Angle Stems

Cut stems at a sharp angle for better water absorption and carefully position each at specific angles: Shin leans slightly forward, Soe extends outward, and Hikae is the shortest, balancing the composition.

Arrange In Symmetry

Follow the triangular structure of Shin (tallest), Soe (medium), and Hikae (shortest), ensuring the arrangement looks natural and flowing rather than symmetrical.

Fill & Refine

Add a few smaller flowers or leaves (Jushi) to enhance the arrangement while keeping simplicity and negative space in mind.

Final Touches

Adjust angles, trim excess leaves, and ensure each element feels balanced and harmonious while embracing the beauty of minimalism.
